History of Basketball

Basketball was invented by Dr. James Naismith in 1891 in Springfield, Massachusetts, USA. His goal was to create a game that would keep students active indoors during the winter. What started as a simple game with a soccer ball and a peach basket has now become one of the most popular sports in the world.

Since its invention, basketball has evolved with the introduction of professional leagues like the NBA, which has helped shape the modern game we see today.

The Rise of the NBA

The NBA, founded in 1946, played a major role in globalizing basketball. From legends like Michael Jordan and Magic Johnson to current stars like LeBron James and Stephen Curry, the NBA has produced some of the most iconic players in sports history.

How to Play Basketball

Basketball is played on a rectangular court with two teams of five players each. The goal is to score points by making a shot through the hoop, which is 10 feet above the ground. Players must dribble the ball while moving, pass to teammates, and attempt to score. The opposing team plays defense to stop them from scoring.

Basic Basketball Positions

Basketball players typically play one of five positions: Point Guard, Shooting Guard, Small Forward, Power Forward, or Center. Each position has specific responsibilities, but all players need to have strong skills in dribbling, passing, and shooting.

Basketball Rules
  • Time: Games consist of four quarters, each lasting 12 minutes in professional play (NBA).
  • Scoring: A field goal is worth 2 points, or 3 points if made from beyond the three-point arc. Free throws are worth 1 point each.
  • Fouls: Players can commit personal fouls. After a certain number of fouls, players may be disqualified from the game.
  • Dribbling: Players must dribble the ball while moving. Traveling (moving without dribbling) is a violation.
